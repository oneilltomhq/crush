# AI Agent Engineering Contract Landscape — Raw Research

Compiled from Tavily searches, March 2026. This is working material, not a finished artifact.

---

## Rate Reality

**UK contract market (contractoruk.com, itjobswatch.co.uk):**
- AI Agents: £575/day average
- LLM: £560/day
- RAG: £573/day
- Generative AI: £560/day
- AI contract jobs in UK: 2,867 in 6 months to Feb 2026 (up from 926 same period 2025 — 3x growth)
- AI now 6.88% of all UK contract jobs (was 3.03% a year ago)

**Global freelance rates (jobbers.io, multiple sources):**
- Junior LLM developers: $100-$150/hr
- Senior LLM specialists: $150-$250/hr
- Top practitioners: $200-$300+/hr
- Senior independent AI consultants: $300-$500/hr (projected $350-$600 in 2027)

**Platform rate ranges:**
- Toptal: $65-200+/hr (but 30-100% client markup hidden)
- Lemon.io: $45-120/hr
- Arc.dev: $60-100+/hr
- Gun.io: $75-110/hr
- Upwork: $10-150+/hr (widest range, lowest floor)

**Key signal:** "Agentic AI" is the steepest salary growth of any AI subcategory tracked (Kore1 staffing guide). Barely existed as a job category 2 years ago, now everywhere.

## Channels — Ranked by Probable Signal for Agentic Engineering

### Tier 1: High signal, worth investing in

**LinkedIn**
- User already identified as high-signal
- Best reach for full-time; also has contract roles
- High applicant volume, low average signal — but can be gamed with specific positioning
- Tractable with auth_browse (user's logged-in sessions)

**X/Twitter**
- User identified as "cool kid" landscape with high-signal folks
- Hard to search systematically via Tavily — it's a network/relationship channel, not a job board
- Value is in building visibility + warm intros, not searching listings
- Tractable with auth_browse for reading feeds; real value requires human presence

**AI Engineer Summit (ai.engineer)**
- Conference + community (NYC, Nov 2025 was latest)
- Has a **jobs board**: wf2025.ai.engineer/jobs — companies like ClickHouse, Strobe Power posting agent-related roles
- Speaker/sponsor companies = companies investing in AI agents = potential clients
- Latent.Space Discord connected to this community
- Tractable: jobs page is scrapable, speaker list is scrapable → company leads

**HN Who's Hiring**
- Monthly threads with direct company posts
- March 2026 thread has agent-related roles (e.g. "AI-agent-optimized monorepo" at Strobe Power, Brooklyn)
- hnhiring.com aggregates these
- Highly tractable: plain text, structured, searchable

### Tier 2: Viable platforms, different dynamics

**WellFound (AngelList)**
- Strong for startup-oriented engineers
- Lower noise than LinkedIn
- Mostly full-time/onsite, but startup world is more flexible on structure
- Tractable: browse/search their listings

**Toptal**
- Vetted network, top 3%
- High rates but platform takes huge markup (30-100%)
- You apply to *Toptal*, not to jobs — they match you to clients
- Requires passing their screening process first

**Lemon.io**
- Startup-focused, lower rates than Toptal
- 4-year vetting process
- Good for volume, less for premium positioning

**Arc.dev**
- AI-powered matching, pre-screened pool
- $60-100+/hr range — below what senior agentic work commands
- Better for companies hiring than for independent contractors seeking premium

### Tier 3: Volume channels, low signal

**Upwork**
- Massive platform, AI is their featured skill category for 2026
- "If 2025 marked the emergence of AI agents, 2026 will be the year they are operationalized" (Upwork research)
- Rate race to bottom for generalist work; premium niches exist but hard to find
- Tractable but noisy

**Freelancer.com**
- Similar to Upwork, even more race-to-bottom
- Not worth investing in for premium agentic engineering

### Other mentions worth noting

- **Jobbers.io** — 0% commission platform, claims to save significantly vs Toptal
- **Gun.io** — $75-110/hr, developer-focused
- **Contra** — commission-free freelance platform
- **SolidGigs** — curated lead aggregator
- **Turing** — AI-matched, $100-200/hr, enterprise-focused
- **Levels.fyi job board** — self-selects for engineers tracking comp (more senior)
- **Harnham** — specialist AI/data recruiter

## Demand Landscape

**Where agentic AI work is happening:**

1. **Startups building agent products** — the ai.engineer ecosystem, YC companies, etc.
2. **Enterprises adopting agent tooling** — ServiceNow ("AI agent control tower"), Snowflake, LinkedIn (staff AI engineers building agent eval frameworks)
3. **Platform companies** — building agent infrastructure (Temporal.io for workflow orchestration, ClickHouse for agent data)
4. **Consulting/agency** — firms staffing AI projects for enterprise clients
5. **Open-source ecosystem** — less contract work, more reputation building

**CB Insights signals:** M&A in agents is distributed across agent builder platforms, vertical players, and recruiting. Platform companies acquiring to defend position.

## What's NOT in this research (gaps)

- **Direct outreach effectiveness** — cold email/DM to founders vs. platform applications. No data from Tavily on this.
- **UK-specific contract landscape** — most data is US-centric. contractoruk.com has rates but not deep agentic-specific analysis.
- **Community channels** — Discord servers, Slack groups, specific subreddits where work gets shared informally. Tavily can't see these.
- **Recruiter landscape** — which agencies specialize in agentic AI contracting specifically.
- **Win rate** — what % of applications/outreach actually converts. No public data.
- **The auth_browse gap** — LinkedIn and X are high-signal but require logged-in access to really work. Can't fully research these channels without the user's browser.
